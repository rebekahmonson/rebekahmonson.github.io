Over the past decade, I've had the privilege of building local newsletter brands across the country through WhereBy.Us. From Miami's The New Tropic to Seattle's The Evergrey, we've learned a lot about what it takes to build and sustain a community through email.

## Email Isn't Dead

Despite what the headlines might tell you, email remains one of the most powerful tools for community building. Here's why:

1. **Direct access** — Unlike social media, you own the relationship with your subscribers
2. **Higher engagement** — People check their email daily, and they choose to be there
3. **Intentionality** — Email subscribers are opting in, signaling genuine interest

## The Daily Habit

One of our key insights was the power of the daily email. By showing up in someone's inbox every morning, we became part of their routine. We weren't just a media brand; we became a companion to their morning coffee.

This consistency builds trust and deepens engagement in ways that sporadic posting on social media never could.

## Local + Curious

Our formula was simple: focus on curious locals who want to be more connected to their city. These aren't people looking for breaking news—they're looking for context, community, and things to do.

By narrowing our focus geographically and psychographically, we could create content that felt personal and relevant.

## What's Next

This experience led us to build Letterhead, software that helps anyone create and monetize their own community newsletter. We believe the future of media is distributed, local, and community-owned.
